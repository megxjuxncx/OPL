Curation of OER

Open Educational Resources are freely accessible and openly licensed materials that educators and students can use, adapt and share. These diverse resources are aimed at promoting equitable access to education. Thus, establishing a method to curate OER shared by teachers is essential.

Steps to create OER

1. Objectives and scope.
2. Identify existing resources.
3. Select a platform.
4. Design the platform.
5. Curate resources.
6. Content licensing and compliance.
7. Pilot test of our OER Library.
8. Launch and promotion.
9. Update and maintain the OER Library.

ITS & Human support service

Intelligent Tutoring Systems are educational platforms that offer personalized and adaptive feedback to students in real time, simulating a human tutor. They use artificial intelligence to monitor progress and adjust content to improve learning. Some examples are Cognitive Tutor, AutoTutor and ALEKS. However, a human team would be available to assist users if needed.

Steps to design and implement an ITS

1. Develop the model.
2. Design the pedagogical model.
3. Pilot test of the model.

Hackathon

A hackathon can be an innovative way to foster collaboration, problem-solving and the creation of new teaching resources. It would be used when a resource that is not uploaded in the repository needs to be created.

Steps to implement a hackathon

1. Identify the specific needs of a group of educators.
2. Present these needs to a team of technical specialists.
3. Organize sessions.
4. Provide support from the OER Library.
5. Provide support regarding licenses and possible patents.

Teaching experimentation space

This section aims to foster innovation and creativity in teaching practices. It serves as a space where educators can explore and experiment with various digital tools that facilitate the creation of educational content.

Features of the space

1. Library of free apps, websites and platforms for educators to record content.
2. Live sessions, tutorials, experiences and best practices.
